\section{Overview}

Historical Simulation (HS) is the simplest and most transparent VaR
methodology. It makes no assumptions about the return distribution: VaR
is estimated directly as an empirical quantile of the observed portfolio
return series. The method is therefore fully non-parametric and requires
no model calibration.

\textbf{Engine key:} \texttt{'hs'} \\
\textbf{Source:} \texttt{risk\_analytics/risk\_engines.py}, lines 228--250

%-----------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Methodology}

\subsection{Portfolio Return Construction}

Given a matrix of asset returns $\mathbf{R} \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times N}$
and a weight vector $\mathbf{w} \in \mathbb{R}^N$, the portfolio return
series is:
\begin{equation}
r_t^{pf} = \sum_{i=1}^{N} w_i \, r_{i,t}, \qquad t = 1, \ldots, T
\end{equation}
Only the most recent $T$ observations are used (controlled by the
\texttt{window} parameter, default $T = 250$).

\subsection{Value-at-Risk}

The 1-day VaR at tail probability $\alpha$ is the negative of the
$\alpha$-quantile of the empirical distribution:
\begin{equation}
\widehat{\text{VaR}}_\alpha = -Q_\alpha\!\left(\{r_t^{pf}\}_{t=1}^T\right)
\end{equation}
where $Q_\alpha(\cdot)$ denotes the empirical $\alpha$-th quantile.
A positive value indicates a loss. Default quantiles:
$\alpha \in \{0.05, 0.01, 0.001\}$.

\subsection{Conditional Value-at-Risk (Expected Shortfall)}

CVaR is the expected loss conditional on the loss exceeding the VaR
threshold:
\begin{equation}
\widehat{\text{CVaR}}_\alpha
= -\frac{1}{|\mathcal{T}_\alpha|} \sum_{t \in \mathcal{T}_\alpha} r_t^{pf}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{T}_\alpha = \{t : r_t^{pf} \leq Q_\alpha\}$
is the set of dates where the portfolio return falls at or below the
VaR quantile.

\subsection{Holding-Period Scaling}

Multi-day estimates are obtained via the square-root-of-time rule:
\begin{equation}
\widehat{\text{VaR}}_\alpha^{(H)} = \widehat{\text{VaR}}_\alpha^{(1)} \cdot \sqrt{H}
\qquad
\widehat{\text{CVaR}}_\alpha^{(H)} = \widehat{\text{CVaR}}_\alpha^{(1)} \cdot \sqrt{H}
\end{equation}

\section{Parameters}

\begin{tabular}{llll}
\toprule
Parameter & Type & Default & Description \\
\midrule
\texttt{window} & int & 250 & Lookback window (number of trading days) \\
\texttt{qtls}   & list & [0.05, 0.01, 0.001] & Tail probability levels \\
\texttt{holding\_period} & int & 1 & Forecast horizon in days \\
\texttt{decay}  & float & 0.94 & Defined but not used in HS (reserved) \\
\texttt{verbose} & bool & True & Print model setup information \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\medskip
\textbf{Note:} The \texttt{decay} parameter is accepted for interface
consistency but has no effect in the Historical Simulation engine.

%-----------------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Advantages and Limitations}

\begin{tabular}{p{6cm} p{8cm}}
\toprule
Advantages & Limitations \\
\midrule
No distributional assumptions & Does not capture volatility clustering \\
Simple and transparent & VaR at 99.9\% requires $>1000$ observations \\
Captures fat tails empirically & Backward-looking; slow to adapt to new regimes \\
No calibration required & Square-root scaling assumes i.i.d.\ returns \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
